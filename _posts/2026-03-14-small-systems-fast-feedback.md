I keep coming back to the same preference: start with the smallest system that lets you learn quickly.

A lot of projects fail in the planning phase because they try to model the entire future before they have touched reality. The safer move is usually to cut the scope until the loop is tight enough to run every day.

Fast feedback changes the quality of decisions. It is easier to fix a bad assumption on day two than on month four. Small systems also force clarity. If something only works when surrounded by a large amount of ceremony, it probably does not really work yet.

This does not mean thinking small. It means proving the direction before scaling the machinery.
