title: "Biology Needs Better Tools"
date: 2026-03-18 08:30:00 +0000
categories: notes
---

Biology still has too many workflows that depend on patience, tribal knowledge, and luck.

That is partly because the science is hard, but it is also because the software around it is often treated as an afterthought. A surprising amount of real work still happens in spreadsheets, PDFs, and improvised scripts that only one person understands.

There is a large gap between what modern software can do and what many labs or biotech teams actually get to use day to day. Better tooling would not make the science trivial, but it would make the work more legible, repeatable, and easier to share.

The opportunity is not just in big platforms. Sometimes the useful thing is a narrow tool that removes one recurring source of friction. Good software earns trust by making the boring parts less fragile.
