The act of writing is still one of the best debugging tools available.

When an idea is vague, it can hide inside your head for a long time. The moment you try to explain it cleanly, the weak parts become obvious. That is useful whether you are working through a technical design, a scientific claim, or a messy opinion.

Public writing adds one more constraint: it has to make sense to someone who was not present for the entire internal monologue. That pressure is healthy. It encourages precision.

So a lot of this site will be exactly that: notes written clearly enough that I can return to them later and still trust what I meant.
