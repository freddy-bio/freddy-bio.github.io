date: 2026-03-05 08:00:00 +0000
categories: notes
---

Bad interfaces do not just waste time. They change the quality of the work itself.

When a tool is awkward, people avoid using it fully. They skip annotations, postpone clean-up, and stop recording intermediate results. Over time that turns into missing context, fragile handoffs, and avoidable confusion about what actually happened.

This matters in science just as much as in software. A notebook, pipeline, or internal tool is not separate from the work. It shapes what gets noticed, what gets repeated, and what gets lost.

Good interfaces make the right action feel easy at the moment it matters.
